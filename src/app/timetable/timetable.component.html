This app contains data from UofT's old Arts and Science timetable as well as a visual timetable. Access the updated version of this app HERE, which contains more faculities and information on each course.

Search for a course designator (first 3 letters of a course code), or multiple seperated by commas, up to a maximum of four. There is no option to search an individual course or search by a course name, so this is the only way to do it. An example of a search entry would be ENG, CHM, BIO.

Here are some notes:

  • Source of course information
  • If you don't know what an icon does, hover over it to see what it means.
  • Click on the top part (colored) of a course card to show its description.
  • Click on the bottom part (white) of a course card to open up its timings.
  • AVOID SELECTING COURSES THAT END ON AN XX:30 - IT MAY CRASH THE TIMETABLE
  • Click on the checkbox on a timing to add the course into the timetable. You may add as many courses as you want.
  • You CANNOT ADD COURSES THAT CONFLICT!! This app won't let you. This is due to technical reasons.
  • Orange represents the F session, blue represents the S session, and purple represents the Y session code. You can see which sessions a course is offered on the middle left of a course card - look at those color strips. Hover over them if you want to know what they mean.
  • If the size of the timetable is too small, you can resize it by dragging on that box located on the bottom middle of your screen. The box with the two diagonal lines, specifically, if you're using windows.
  • This site MAY NOT be the most mobile friendly.
  • Reload the page to clear the timetable. There is NO WAY to save your timetable entries, so the best method of exporting is to take a screenshot.
  • If you want to print this timetable, you should screenshot it, then print your screenshot. There is no option to print this timetable at the moment.
  • This timetable is NOT ALWAYS UP TO DATE with the latest UofT A&S course information. This timetable is accurate as of {{lastUpdateString}}.
  • The following colors map to the following breath requirements:
    • BR1 - RED
    • BR2 - BLUE
    • BR3 - YELLOW
    • BR4 - GREEN
    • BR5 - PURPLE
